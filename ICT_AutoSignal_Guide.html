// 02 ‚Äî MARKET STRUCTURE

BOS & CHoCH

The foundation of every signal. The indicator uses ta.pivothigh / ta.pivotlow with a configurable lookback to identify swing points, then classifies each break.

BOS ‚Äî Break of Structure

Trend continuation signal. A bullish BOS fires when price breaks above the prior swing high in the direction of the existing bias. Drawn as a dashed lime line ‚Äî confirms the trend is intact.

// Bullish BOS logic
bos_bull = bull_bias and
  high > last_sh and
  high[1] <= last_sh

CHoCH ‚Äî Change of Character

Trend reversal signal. Fires when price breaks a swing level against the current bias. Immediately flips bull_bias ‚Äî changes which OBs and signals are valid from that bar forward.

// Bearish CHoCH logic
choch_bear = bull_bias and
  not na(last_sl) and
  low < last_sl
‚ö†Ô∏è
Lookback setting matters. Default is 10 bars. Lower values (5‚Äì7) = more sensitive, more signals, more noise. Higher values (15‚Äì20) = cleaner structure, fewer but stronger BOS/CHoCH labels.
// 03 ‚Äî ORDER BLOCKS

Order Block Detection

Order Blocks (OBs) represent the last candle before a strong impulse move ‚Äî the price level where institutional orders were placed. The indicator auto-detects them using an ATR-filtered impulse threshold.

Bullish OB

Triggered when a bullish impulse candle (body > 0.5√ó ATR) is preceded by a bearish candle. That bearish candle's range becomes the OB zone. Price returning here signals a potential long.

Bearish OB

Triggered when a bearish impulse candle is preceded by a bullish candle. That bullish candle's range becomes the OB zone. Price returning here signals a potential short.

// Impulse detection ‚Äî ATR filtered
bull_impulse = close > open and (close - open) > atr14 * 0.5
bear_impulse = close < open and (open - close) > atr14 * 0.5

// OB zone = the candle BEFORE the impulse
ob_top = math.max(open[1], close[1])
ob_bottom = math.min(open[1], close[1])
üí°
The impulse ATR multiplier of 0.5 can be increased in code to 0.75 or 1.0 to only mark the strongest institutional candles ‚Äî reduces OB clutter significantly on fast markets like NQ.
// 04 ‚Äî FAIR VALUE GAPS

Fair Value Gap Detection

An FVG is a three-candle imbalance where price moves so fast that a gap forms between candle[0] and candle[2]. Institutions use these levels as magnets ‚Äî price typically returns to fill the gap before continuing.

Bullish FVG

Condition: low[0] > high[2]

Gap above two bars ago. Price moved up so fast it left an unfilled zone. Teal box marks the imbalance area between high[2] and low[0].

Bearish FVG

Condition: high[0] < low[2]

Gap below two bars ago. Price dropped so fast it left an imbalance. Orange box marks the zone between high[0] and low[2].

Using FVG as Confluence

Enable Require FVG Confluence in the Entry Signals settings. A long signal only fires if price is simultaneously inside a bullish OB AND inside a bullish FVG. Fewer signals, much higher probability.

‚ö†Ô∏è
On fast-moving markets like MNQ during NY Open, FVGs can form and fill within the same session. FVG mitigation uses a close-through check ‚Äî on 1m or 2m charts this fires frequently. Use 5m as your primary FVG reference timeframe.
// 05 ‚Äî KILL ZONES

Kill Zones

ICT Kill Zones are the high-probability time windows when institutional volume is highest. The indicator supports three sessions with background shading and an optional signal filter.

London Open
02:00 ‚Äì 05:00 UTC-5
Best for Euro pairs + NQ overnight. Often sets the day's high or low via liquidity sweep before reversal.
NY Open
07:00 ‚Äì 10:00 UTC-5
Most powerful session for futures. RTH open at 9:30 EST is inside this window. Highest OB + FVG hit rate. Primary signal window for ES/NQ/MNQ.
NY Lunch
12:00 ‚Äì 13:00 UTC-5
Low volume chop window. Disabled by default. Enable only for afternoon reversal setups ‚Äî not recommended for prop accounts.

Candle Confirmation Patterns

Bullish Engulfing

Close > open, closes above prior high, opens at or below prior close. Strong conviction candle showing buyers absorbed all sell pressure.

Bullish Pin Bar (Hammer)

Lower wick > 2√ó body, small upper wick (<0.25 ATR). Rejection of lower prices ‚Äî smart money absorbed sell orders at the OB zone.

Bearish Engulfing

Close < open, closes below prior low, opens at or above prior close. Sellers overwhelmed buyers ‚Äî bearish OB retest confirmed.

Bearish Pin Bar (Shooting Star)

Upper wick > 2√ó body, small lower wick. Institutional supply entered aggressively at the OB ‚Äî rejection of higher prices.

Zone membership uses low (longs) and high (shorts) ‚Äî not close. A signal can fire mid-wick even if the close is outside the zone. For stricter entries, manually confirm the close is within the OB box before executing.
